Model Performance Report: Comparative Performance Analysis of Machine Learning Stacks in Healthcare Predictive Analytics

Abstract

This report provides a detailed empirical evaluation of machine learning models tailored for healthcare predictive analytics, focusing on their accuracy, robustness, and reliability in predicting risk, outlier, and scope probabilities.

Introduction

The document builds upon prior research to evaluate the effectiveness of advanced machine learning models in healthcare analytics, using a comprehensive set of performance metrics for a robust comparison.

Methodology

The study employed an enhanced cross-validation methodology complemented by performance metrics such as the F1 score, confusion matrix analysis, and learning curve assessments to gauge the models' capabilities in addressing overfitting and ensuring generalization.

Results and Discussion

The study identified three machine learning models that exhibited superior performance. The RandomQuadraticEncoder model demonstrated an accuracy of 91.3%, with a balanced F1 score, indicating a strong precision-recall equilibrium. The AutoIsolationForestTransformer model showed a notable accuracy of 94.1% and a high area under the precision-recall curve, reflecting its efficacy in distinguishing between normal and anomalous instances. Lastly, the CatTransformer model recorded an accuracy of 92.3%, with a confusion matrix revealing a high true positive rate and a low false positive rate, underscoring its precision in classifying out-of-scope activities.

Conclusion

The analysis confirms the suitability of the RandomQuadraticEncoder, AutoIsolationForestTransformer, and CatTransformer models for healthcare predictive analytics. Their high accuracy rates and balanced performance metrics suggest that these models are well-equipped for operational deployment, providing reliable and robust predictions for risk assessment, anomaly detection, and scope probability in healthcare claims.
